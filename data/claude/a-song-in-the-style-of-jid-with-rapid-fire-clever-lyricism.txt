**"Syllable Monsoon"** — 88 bars at 140 BPM (~2:31)

Key design choices for the JID style:

- **140 BPM** — fast tempo matching JID's energetic delivery
- **E minor** key with dark Em9/G^7/Am7/B7b9 progression — moody, Atlanta-influenced
- **Complex hi-hat programming** — rapid-fire rolls (`hh*6`, double-time triggers via `.every(3, x => x.fast(2))`) mimicking trap production with rhythmic variation
- **Hard 808 bass** — distorted sawtooth sub-bass with syncopated patterns and slides between E/G/A/B
- **Rapid melodic runs** — 8-note pentatonic sequences on the melody voice simulate JID's machine-gun flow and syllable density
- **Boom-bap meets trap drums** — kick patterns alternate between sparse and stuttered (`bd ~ [~ bd] bd`), snares double up in choruses
- **Sparse string counter-melody** in choruses only — atmospheric tension without cluttering the "vocal space"
- **Sub-bass drone** anchoring the low end throughout
